Ethos of Permaculture

Permaculture begins with the recognition that humanity's way of life has tended towards overconsumption, inequality and alienation from nature, and that sustainable changes to our role on earth need to be made.

Permaculture applies an ecological view to our approach to design, consciously crafting our relationship with nature. It provides tools to consider the health and productivity of all communities, both human and otherwise, in decision making.

At its most practical, permaculture's goal is to guide the creation of edible landscapes. These landscapes should mirror naturalized diversity and foster a self-managed system of plants, animals and, humans.

The creation of these systems should account for all stakeholders, involve minimal energy input, and restore and conserve local ecosystems. At all scales, from garden to the globe, designing for ecological health and self-reliance are what permaculture seeks to achieve.
